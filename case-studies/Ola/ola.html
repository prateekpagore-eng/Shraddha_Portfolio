Ola Redesign

App Redesign • 2024

OLA is one of India's most widely used cab booking platforms — but its app experience didn't quite match the scale of the product. This was a conceptual redesign project aimed at reimagining OLA's mobile UI with a sharper visual identity, better hierarchy, and a dark-first design system that feels modern, premium and intuitive.

Dark Mode · UI Redesign · Conceptual · Mobility

The Challenge

OLA serves millions of users daily but like any product at scale, there's always room to evolve. As user expectations around mobile experiences continue to rise, there's a growing opportunity to evolve OLA's app into something that feels more intuitive, accessible and seamlessly woven into daily life.


The goal: refine the visual language, reduce friction in the booking flow, introduce a dark mode for better usability across conditions, and explore smarter touchpoints that bring OLA closer to its users without them having to open the app.

The Approach

User Interviews Conducted user interviews to gather insights and identify challenges users encounter with the current app. These are few of the common challenges users face in cab booking:
  • If a user books ride for someone else there's no option to share contact of driver and OTP to the person.
  • In the case of a niche address, users may find it frustrating that the app does not capture the precise location.
  • The driver's frequent tardiness is frustrating.
  • Even when there are 10 drivers nearby, none accept the ride, resulting in wasted time.
  • Initially after accepting the ride, driver is late or is unreachable leading potential cancellation charges for us.
  • Prices are fixed, and users do not have the option to negotiate for their desired price.
Analytics Data
  • Vehicle quality is poor — Several users said that Ola cabs are old, smelly, and air conditioner is not functional.
  • Pricing is high — Users have reported unexpected charges and fare discrepancies.
  • Some users have reported frequent cancellations, there is a deficiency in service. A survey of 65,000 consumers found that 71% of them say ride cancellations are a problem.
  • Driver's behavior is rude — Certain users experienced that drivers cancel rides often, when asked about payment methods, and do not cooperate.

The Solution

Clear Visual Hierarchy

Limited decorative features, attention to typography & overall clarity.

Minimal Dark Mode UI

The thoughtful use of dark mode makes the interface look clean, classy, fresh and aesthetically pleasing in general.

Subtle Roundness on UI Elements

Making the interface look more organic and user friendly.

Real-life Photography

It relates to real-life experiences on an emotional level.

Focus on Contrast

With enough contrast we can easily differentiate background from the elements, understand the visual hierarchy and use the interface with ease.
